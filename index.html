This is Me.....

My name is Michael Fehdrau. I was born deaf into an American-German family and grew up learning both in deaf schools, mainstream hearing schools, and with a speech therapist for 16 years, which helped me speak clearly even though my first language is ASL.
As a child, I dreamed of being an engineer or architect, inspired by my grandfather who built homes. Life took me in a different direction, and I later studied fashion design, creating clothes for all.
Over the years, I’ve worked various jobs and discovered a love for technology. Web design and coding sparked my curiosity about how things function and connect.
Outside of work, I enjoy traveling, painting, drawing, cooking, sightseeing, and visiting museums.
Most people assume I’m hearing, but the truth is—I’m proudly deaf.

Becoming AI Builder.

My goal is to become an AI builder and a software engineer on the side. I’m working to learn coding well enough to build functional systems, and — if I get the results I want — I plan to challenge myself with machine learning.
When AI first hit the public eye, many people panicked or worried it would take jobs and change everything. I don’t see it that way. AI can be a helpful companion that supports our work and makes life better when used responsibly. It’s not cheating; it’s a powerful tool that sits by your side and amplifies what you can do.
ChatGPT was my first real introduction to AI, and it sparked my curiosity. As I learn more and watch others use it, I’m starting to like it more and more. Technology is one of the biggest markets today, and I believe mastering these tools will open meaningful opportunities and help me grow in my career.

Experiences that I Learned.

All my life, I’ve loved creating — from drawing and painting to drafting houses and buildings. Those early hobbies shaped my path. I earned an AA degree in Fashion Design and later a BFA in Visual Communication. Finding work in fashion or design wasn’t easy, but I started from the bottom as a freelancer, knowing I had to begin somewhere.
When I moved to New York City, I pursued both fashion and graphic/web design. Most of my opportunities came in fashion, where I could sew, sketch, and draft clothing patterns. That became the core of my career in NYC.
Still, I never stopped learning. I explored technology online, earning certificates in UX design, HTML5, CSS3, and even a bit of JavaScript and Python. My curiosity drives me to keep building skills, and today my focus is learning AI — to see where it can take me and how I can grow into a successful future.
